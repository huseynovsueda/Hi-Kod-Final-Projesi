SpaceX will launch a new mission to Mars

SpaceX plans to launch an uncrewed mission to Mars in 2022 to investigate water resources, mining and life support infrastructure. Work is being done to carry out the same mission with a crew in 2024.


Roscosmos space tours project

Russian agency Roscosmos has partnered with Space Adventures to create a new tourist destination in space. Within the scope of the project, a "hotel-module" will be added to the International Space Station. The Russian Space Agency reached an agreement with the Russian company RKK Energiya for the production of a module of approximately 6.5 square meters with four rooms, a medical aid unit and a common living area. It is stated that Roscosmos is preparing to offer space tourist candidates the option of staying in this module for a week, 15 days or a month, starting from 2022. A shortened version of the two-year training given to cosmonauts will be offered to candidates. It is claimed that ticket prices will be 40 million dollars for a 15-day trip and 60 million dollars for a one-month trip.


China will establish a lunar base

According to the news in the New York Times, China will establish a base on the Moon in 2022 and will send astronauts to the base by the end of 2020.


Hubble Telescope will be launched

The James Webb Space Telescope, presented as the successor of the Hubble Telescope, will be sent into space on October 31, 2021. The launch, which was previously planned for 2019, was postponed to 2021 due to some production errors. Thanks to its advanced optical equipment, the James Webb Telescope will be able to examine the situation 200 million years after the "Big Bang", which caused the formation of the universe approximately 13.7 billion years ago.


Turkish flag will meet the Moon

Turkish Space Agency (TUA) plans to send an unmanned vehicle to the Moon in 2023, on the 100th anniversary of the Republic. The national and unique hybrid rocket engine, which is planned to be used in the spacecraft for a hard landing on the Moon, made its first firing. The tests were completed successfully. Both engines operated smoothly for the planned duration.
